The Gadi Lohar's Cart

While roaming down, lohar community took to carts and that's why the name, Gadi(Cart) Lohar. The cart typically consist of a back shelter, with a 4 wheels and one extended one for them to move and balance. It usually pulled by a donkey. There's also a(hook attached below the cart for them to keep their pets handy while walking.

The Gadia Lohar School

On 6 April, 1958 post India's Independence, the then PM Nehru, called all the Gadi Lohar community and asked them to let go off their pledge as it's a new India now and there's no one to fight against. It's a democracy. He even set up a school expecially for thr Gadi Lohar community and asked them to move on in their life. Some did, some didn't. Till date, the school functions but the precentage of the Gadi Lohar children is very low, even after being absoultely free. Some don't want to send their kids to school, others can't given the income contribution each child gives to the famiy working as the lohar.

Our guide in the Ochdi, Village

This 70-year old man called Basiram in Ochdi was our host. He told us all the stories, and made us meet with some other locals. He has been actively involved to uplift his community. The moment he heard about us, he came to us and then showed us around introducing all the challenges that the lohars in the Ochdi faces. But he also told us about the rich culture that these people follow and the importance of Maharana Pratap and the pledge. He spoke a lot. In the end, he even offered us some Prasad(sweet offering made ot God).

The Kalki Devi Temple

After having a few hours conversation with the community at the Ochdi village near Chittorgarh, we went straight to their Kuldevi(family deity), called the Kalki Devi. The temple is beside the magnanimous Chittorgarh fort and boasts of a rihc history.

Ochdi Village, Chittrogarh

Post meeting the community in Chittorgarh, we went to meet some more people in a village 10 kms away from Chittor called Ochdi. Locals told us to go there and meet more lohars. There are over 300 living in the village.

There are some Kuccha House too!

Dressed up

Women of the community are always dressed in traditional ornaments. There's a speical emphasis given to the nose piercing and the toe ring here.

Pucca house

Due to some local activists in the past, some families in the village are given a monthly pension of 500 INR per month. Some are given just 100 INR per month. Depsite efforts, some got the houses as promised and others didn't. Out of 300 families living here, majority lives in houses made by sand or teen shed, if they can afford to at all.

In the news!

Due to efforts from a local activists now and then, there had been some support given, and talks about the community in the past. There's a temple of Bhairav in the village. All the newspaper cuttings are pasted on a wall and displayed to anyone who is curious to know about the status and jounrey of the people living here. There were cuttings of news as old as of 2002 as well.

Watch the difference

Some people had given lands and have been provided with basic houses too. Some are not. There have been no pattern, but just the sociopolitical drama.

"It stinks"

They told me that they have been complaning about this sewage leakage for over three months now. It's monsoon now, and often the sewage water flows inside the homes. "It stinks, and stinks badly.", they said.
